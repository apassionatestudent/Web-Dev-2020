Brief Overview of Being Sleepy z z z z
As we're awake doing the daily stuffs that we do, our bodies breaking down ATP Adenosine Triphosphate which results to adenosine, a by-product of ATP Adenosine Triphosphate. These adenosine will go to adenosine receptors and that's what will make us drowsy, tired, etc. in short, to be sleepy. The more adenosine, the more drowsy can we potentially be. This fact can potentially hinders us to study well because we can't focus sharply due to the tiredness we feel. If we can't study well, then we might not learn new things depending upon what topic we're studying of.


Brief Overview on How Caffeine Works ☕
Caffeine and adenosine are more or less similar in structure. This fact makes Caffeine to be the best impostor of adenosine! By being so, Caffeine goes to the adenosine receptors in our body and blocking any adenosine to come into. adenosine makes us tired, Caffeine is otherwise. Caffeine is simply the contradiction of tiredness. This will make us feel alert, fast heartbeats, energetic, etc. Coffee can help us in our studies and I'm drinking coffee for that very purpose. Now some of you might be nervous in drinking even just 1mL of coffee due to some rumors about it. The rumors about coffee are just rumors and unproven. Besides, there's also called the Nocebo Effect which makes us feel sicker due to our beliefs that something is harmful. I won't explain much about it and I encourage you to study about it to have more acquaintance. Although Caffeine can be harmful, only on large doses(depending upon body weight) a day. So grab a cup of coffee and drink, then enjoy studying! ;)

About

What is Networking?

Networking in technological terms, is the process of sending and receiving of data whether in a wired or a wireless medium. They're composed of various equipments dedicated to its cause like switches, routers, etc.


Types of Networks

The types of networks define on how a particular type cover an area geographically.


PAN

PAN stands for Personal Area Network. It's used for connecting devices such as phones, tablets, laptops , etc. in a close proximity to each other. They're connected using Bluetooth, Infrared, NFC(Near Field Communicator) and it can also connect through a wired medium like a USBUniversal Serial Bus cable. They're often used for transferring small files like musics, videos, and so on.


LAN

LAN stands for Local Area Network. This network is composed of devices such as computers, servers, switches, printers, etc. that are located on the same building like inside a house or a small office. The most common type of LAN is called Ethernet LAN which used Ethernet cables.


WLAN

WLAN stands for Wireless Local Area Network. As the name implies, it's a LAN which transfers and receives data in a wireless medium. Also take note that it uses some cables though that are used by the modem and the router but it generally sends and receives data in wireless way.


CAN

CAN stands for Campus Area Network. It's a network that joins two or more LANs together within a limited area. A CAN could be a school that has multiple buildings in the same area that are connected to each other to create a larger network.


MAN

MAN stands for Metropolitan Area Network. It's a network larger than a CAN. This network spans to several buildings in a city or town. They're usually connected using Fiber optics so the data will be transferred much faster.


SAN

SAN stands for Storage Area Network. This is a high-speed network that stores and provides access to large amounts of data. It's a dedicated network used for data storage. It's composed of disk arrays, switches and servers.


WAN

WAN stands for Wide Area Network. It's the largest type of network. This spans to countries, continents and even the entire globe. The internet is the best example of a WAN.
